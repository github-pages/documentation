Managing VAT rates

On the Manage VAT rates page, you can perform the following tasks:

  • Enable VAT rate support for your organization.
  • Define a default VAT rate that applies to all prices for all customers.
  • Define a country-specific VAT rate for every country where you want to sell your services.
  • Define a customer-specific VAT rate, for example, in case a customer organization has a subsidiary located in another country than its parent organization.

The VAT rate settings have the following effects on the cost calculation for a customer:

  • If VAT rate support is disabled, prices are calculated as net prices; no VAT is added to the overall costs.
  • A customer-specific VAT rate takes priority over any default or country-specific VAT rate.
  • The country-specific VAT rate for the country where the customer organization is located is applied to the cost calculation when no customer-specific VAT rate is defined.
  • The default VAT rate is used in all other cases.

The VAT rate does not affect any price model elements. The calculated VAT amount is added to the overall costs and results in the gross price to be paid by a customer. Be aware that you are responsible for setting the correct VAT rates.

If you grant a reseller the permission to sell your services, the customers of your reseller are not affected by the VAT rate you define. Any cost calculation for a reseller's customers results in net prices. If you grant a broker the permission to offer your services, the VAT rates you define are used.

To manage VAT rates:

  • Enable the VAT rate support for your organization by selecting the corresponding check box. When enabled, usage charges for subscriptions are calculated as gross prices. This setting applies to all prices for all your customers.

    To disable the VAT rate support, clear the check box. When VAT rate support is disabled, you cannot specify any default, country-specific, or customer-specific VAT rates; any usage charges are calculated as net prices.

    Be aware that disabling the VAT rate support removes the values for all VAT rates you have defined.

  • In the Default VAT rate (%) field, enter a value to be set as the default VAT rate for your organization.

    This rate is used in case you do not define any country-specific or customer-specific VAT rates.

    Specify a percent value as a decimal number with a maximum of two decimal places.

  • To set a country-specific VAT rate:
    1. Select the country from the Country list. The Country list shows all countries that do not yet have a VAT rate defined.
    2. Enter the VAT rate to be applied for the country in the VAT rate (%) column next to the country name. Specify a percent value as a decimal number with a maximum of two decimal places.
  • To set a customer-specific VAT rate:
    1. Select the customer organization from the Customer list. The list shows the customer organizations that do not yet have a VAT rate defined.
    2. Enter the VAT rate to be applied for the customer organization in the VAT rate (%) column next to the customer name. Specify a percent value as a decimal number with a maximum of two decimal places.
  • To change a VAT rate, change the percent value in the corresponding field.

To store the VAT rate settings you have defined, click Save.
