Defining a subscription price model

On the Define for subscription page, you can define or update a price model for a specific subscription of a selected customer. You can define subscription price models for your direct customers and for your brokers' customers.

The subscription price model you define or update takes priority over the service price model or a customer price model that is defined. However, you cannot change the cost calculation option. Click here for basic information on price models.

If you update a subscription price model, the modified settings are valid for the current and future billing periods. This means that, for example, all user assignments or billable events that occurred in using the subscription in the current billing period before the change will be charged according to the new price model.

Usually, you will change a subscription price model only in agreement with the relevant customer. For changing a subscription price model, it does not matter whether the relevant service is activated or deactivated.

To define a subscription price model:

  1. In the list in the upper page area, select the subscription for which you want to define the price model.

    You can select any active subscription to your services. The subscriptions of your direct customers and your brokers' customers are included.

    Below, you can view which cost calculation option is valid for the selected subscription:

    • Free of charge: The service is free of charge.
    • Pro rata calculation: The costs are calculated based on the exact usage of the service in milliseconds.
    • Per time unit calculation: The costs are calculated for complete time units, for example, whole days or weeks. The time unit is defined under Charges based on.

    If the subscription is free of charge, proceed with Step 6, else with Step 2.

  2. In the Description field, enter a detailed description of the price model. It may, for example, provide details of each element the price model consists of.

    The description is displayed to the customer on a marketplace when he requests details of the service from the service catalog.

    You can specify the description in additional languages later by localizing the price model.

    Note: Make sure to use only the controls provided in the editor for formatting your text. Manually entered HTML tags and references to external style sheets or content may result in errors in the page layout.
  3. Specify the charging conditions. They include the following information:
    • Currency in which a customer is charged for using the service. The supported currencies are defined by the operator. The currency cannot be changed when defining a subscription price model.
    • Charges based on: The time unit for recurring charges. The charges can be on a monthly, weekly, daily, or hourly basis. The option selected here also defines the time unit for per time unit calculation. The time unit cannot be changed when defining a subscription price model.
    • One-time fee to be paid when subscribing to the service. This fee is added to the usage charges for the first billing period.
    • Recurring charge per subscription: The charge to be paid for a subscription on a recurring basis as specified in the Charges based on field.
    • Recurring charge for users: The charge to be paid per time unit users are assigned to a subscription. The time unit is defined in the Charges based on field. For this type of charge, you can specify stepped prices, for example, to grant discounts depending on the amount of time users work with a subscription:
      • Click the field for entering a price. A plus sign appears to its right.
      • Click the plus sign and enter a price per time unit as well as a step limit. The step limit defines the sum of the time units of all user assignments in a billing period up to which the specified price is valid.
      • Proceed with the definition of prices and step limits. Use the plus sign to add a price step, the minus sign to remove a step.
      • The last Price per time unit entry field defines the price to be applied for all time units exceeding the number specified in the last Step limit field.
      Click here for detailed information.
  4. Specify the prices for the billable events defined for the service.

    These include, for example, login and logout by users to the underlying application, the completion of specific transactions, or the creation or deletion of specific data. It depends on the implementation and integration of the underlying application which events are available.

    The available events are listed in the table. In the Description column, you can view their short descriptions.

    For this type of charge, you can specify stepped prices, for example, to grant discounts depending on the number of events that occurred while using the service:
    • Click the field for entering a price. A plus sign appears to its right.
    • Click the plus sign and enter a price per event as well as a step limit. The step limit defines the number of events up to which the price per event is valid.
    • Proceed with the definition of prices per event and step limits. Use the plus sign to add a price step, the minus sign to remove a step.
    • The last Price entry field defines the price to be applied for all events of the selected type exceeding the number specified in the last Step limit field.
    Click here for detailed information.
  5. Specify the prices for parameters and options.

    It depends on the implementation and integration of the underlying application whether and which parameters and options are available.

    The available parameters and options are listed in the table. In the Description column, you can view their short descriptions.

    You can define a price for every parameter and option, and specify whether this price is to be charged per subscription or per user assigned to the subscription. Numeric parameters are a multiplier for the price. For boolean parameters, the multiplier is 1 if the value is true. In all other cases, the multiplier is 0.

    For numeric parameters (parameters with values of type Integer or Long), you can define stepped prices per subscription, for example, to grant volume discounts:
    • Click the field for entering a price. A plus sign appears to its right.
    • Click the plus sign and enter a price as well as a step limit. The step limit defines the value up to which the price is valid.
    • Proceed with the definition of prices and step limits. Use the plus sign to add a price step, a minus sign to remove a step.
    • The last Price entry field defines the price to be applied for all parameter values exceeding the number specified in the last Step limit field.
    Click here for detailed information.
  6. To save the price model, click Save.
  7. To provide the price model's description in another language, click Localize.
  8. To specify prices for roles that are defined for the underlying application, click Define for Service Roles. The price you define for a service role is added to the base price per user in the cost calculation for a billing period.
The price model changes for the subscription take effect immediately.
